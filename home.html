Breaking News: World War III Averted by Coin Toss

In a surprising turn of events, the leaders of the United States, Russia, China, and North Korea have agreed to settle their differences by a simple coin toss, instead of launching nuclear missiles at each other.

The historic decision was made after hours of tense negotiations at the United Nations headquarters in New York, where the four leaders realized that they had no other option but to resort to a game of chance.

"We were running out of time and patience," said US President Joe Biden. "We had to find a way to end this madness before it was too late."

"It was either heads or tails, or fire and fury," said Russian President Vladimir Putin. "We chose the former."

"We respect the outcome of the coin toss," said Chinese President Xi Jinping. "We hope that this will lead to a more peaceful and stable world."

"We are not happy with the result, but we accept it," said North Korean leader Kim Jong-un. "We will not launch any more rockets or missiles."

The coin toss was conducted by UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres, who used a special commemorative coin with the UN logo on one side and a peace symbol on the other. The coin landed on the peace symbol, which meant that the four leaders had to shake hands and call off their war plans.
